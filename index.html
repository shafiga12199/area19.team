Hour of Code Project

How was the project arranged?

By: Maryam Mirili

None of us had in mind that we would have to be responsible for someone other than ourselves. However, life is unpredictable. So, the day we received the homework assignment, we created a Spike Group to chat with team members, with whom we have nothing in common but two more team assignments.Of course, we had disagreements, in fact we still do. However, that is not the point. Moving on you will find out how Shafiga found school where we would finally remove one of the stones from our shoulders.!

How was the school found?

By: Shafiga Alakbarli

At first, the team struggled to find a school because we wanted to run the presentation precisely with a refugee school. Eventually, I found such a type of school and even made an arrangement with the form-master in Fuzuli (Karabakh) Secondary School No.49. Unfortunately, there was a time conflict with that class, due to which the meeting was cancelled. Over a few days of searching, another school was found and a suitable class from Baku School-Lyceum No.299 for conducting an event.

Action

By: Nazrin Gismatli

On the set day of our presentation, all the pupils joined our team, and the meeting started. As it was planned, we all introduced ourselves and where we came from. Polad Bakhishzade made a brief overview regarding ADA University and, in particular, our faculty. Next, Nazrin Gismatli, taking the duty of a tutor, began giving information about code.org and its puzzles. Whilst she was tutoring the students, the other team members were taking notes, looking after the students' overall progress and, in case of possible questions, were answering and filling up the potential gaps in mind of students.

Ending

....

At the end of the meeting, the pupils were asked a few questions, including their perception about the course, whether they took similar ones before. According to what they said, it was their first time when they were taught such type of courses, and, generally, they enjoyed the presentation. Moreover, we hope that we could encourage at least some of them to get deeper knowledge in coding and informatics overall in the future.
